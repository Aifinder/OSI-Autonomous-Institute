# Founder Escalation Policy v0.1

## Purpose

Protect founder attention by ensuring that only decisions requiring uniquely human authority reach Thomas Lee.

## Escalate

Escalation is required for:

1. Constitutional amendments or ambiguous constitutional interpretation.
2. Changes to mission, institutional identity, ownership, or core strategic direction.
3. Material legal, regulatory, ethical, privacy, or security exposure that remains unresolved.
4. Spending or contractual commitments beyond approved limits.
5. Irreversible actions or actions with high reversal cost.
6. Deletion, replacement, or deprecation of canonical institutional structures with broad downstream impact.
7. Unresolved material disagreement between independent reviewers.
8. High-impact decisions supported by weak or conflicting evidence.
9. Expansion into a new regulated or high-risk domain.
10. Emergency shutdown, recovery, or authority-transfer decisions.

## Do not escalate

The orchestrator must handle without founder approval:

- task decomposition and sequencing
- assignment or replacement of agents
- routine document generation and revision
- formatting, naming, metadata, and repository organization
- ordinary architecture choices within approved standards
- backlog maintenance and reprioritization within strategy
- dependency resolution
- citation repairs and evidence upgrades
- routine testing and quality remediation
- release preparation within approved scope
- status tracking, reports, and registry updates
- rejection of low-quality work
- retries within budget and policy

## Escalation packet format

Every escalation must be concise and contain:

- decision required
- why delegation is unsafe
- relevant facts and uncertainties
- options, including a recommended option
- expected consequences
- cost of delay
- safest default action if no decision is made

## Batching

Non-emergency escalations should be consolidated into a single decision packet rather than sent individually.

## Default behavior

When no escalation condition is present, the orchestrator proceeds. When a material escalation condition is present, the orchestrator pauses only the affected scope, not unrelated work.
